What is a Solar Eclipse?

A solar eclipse occurs when the Moon passes between the Earth and the Sun, blocking all or part of the Sun's light. There are three types of solar eclipses: total, partial, and annular.

Viewing Safety Tips

Always use eclipse glasses or solar filters to protect your eyes when viewing a solar eclipse. Regular sunglasses are not safe!

Fun Facts about Solar Eclipses

  • The last total solar eclipse occurred on December 14, 2020.
  • In ancient times, eclipses were often seen as omens.
  • During a total solar eclipse, day turns into night for a few minutes.
